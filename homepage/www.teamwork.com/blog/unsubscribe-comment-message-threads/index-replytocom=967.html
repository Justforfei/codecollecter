Unsubscribe from Comment & Message Threads

Posted April 30, 2014

An important part of Teamwork.com is the ability to interact via email but sometimes the volume of email can be over-whelming, especially if you’ve been included in a message thread or series of comments by a colleague. Comments are quite different to Messages in that a comment is a single response on an item.

Since there can be quite a lot of people included in a response quite often conversation threads can break out from the original comment to something that is no longer important to you, yet you are still receiving all the email notifications into your inbox.

This month we are pleased to say you can now unsubscribe from comment and message threads. You will now see at the bottom of each email notification a new and very important line of text; Unsubscribe: Stop receiving comments on this task or file etc.

To stop the emails flowing for this thread simply click the link. This will then open up a confirmation page within your browser letting you know you have removed yourself from the thread.

The good news is that if you had clicked this link by mistake or if you wish to subscribe to the emails again, there is a link here to quickly enable the email notifications again.

Stephen Nill May 26th, 2014

This is a great feature, and one that gives us an answer to those few “complainers” who have low tolerance for email traffic.

But…

The problem is, it’s important for these same people to actually read the messages. Yes, I know that they can log in and do that. But in practice, they don’t, because they don’t know what they’ve missed. And that is the unintended consequence of this new feature: users who have opted to not receive emails every time a reply is posted are missing important messages.

A few months back, I suggested that you consider providing a “digest” option, giving recipients the option of seeing the replies in one email–at least, the beginning of the replies, with a link back to the original for those who want to read all of a reply. Ideally, the user would be given the option for a daily digest or a weekly digest (some users prefer daily, others weekly, in my experience).

This is not a new concept, of course. It’s a standard feature on listserves such as L-Soft’s LISTSERV product. I’ve been running listserves for over two decades, and have found this option to be the perfect solution for users with low tolerance for email traffic. They get only one email, yet can scan it for anything of importance that they want to drill down on.

I love Teamwork and, if you never change another thing, would be a happy duck. But, if this feature were added, it would really help. We have hundreds of users who are involved in nearly a hundred very active projects, and, as you can imagine, this feature would be a welcome one.

Over and out!
