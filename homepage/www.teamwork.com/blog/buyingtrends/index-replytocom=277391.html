Still Using Paper To Manage Your Projects? You’re not the only one ..

Posted May 14, 2015

Software Advice, a company that matches software buyers with online project management solutions recently produced a great report on Project Management Software buying trends which we found really interesting.

 

Here are some of the more surprising stats that came out of the report:

 

  • 70% of prospective buyers currently use manual methods for their project management needs
  • 64% are in the market for a new solution
  • 58% use a single method for their project management needs
  • Time tracking and task management are the top requested capabilities

Number of Current Project Management methods used.

 

For anyone that already uses project management software religiously, these numbers probably seem a little unusual. As most of you reading this are users who, on a day-to-day basis, structure everything through a productivity or project management tool, it’s difficult to comprehend why anyone would do it any other way.

However, old habits die hard and some people tend to default to what they consider the old reliables – spreadsheets and email. The biggest challenge is the fear of change. The time you have to invest in researching, trialling, and training on new tools can seem like too much hassle in the short term. Despite what these figures say though, we are starting to see more and more companies move to high tech online solutions. Everyday at Teamwork.com we deal with experienced users that are looking for a new solution but also people who are only just beginning to use a project management tool. Something that’s highlighted time and time again is that spreadsheets and email are time consuming, inefficient, and, therefore, not sustainable as a process – people know they need to change, they’re just not sure how to go about it.

Top Requested Web Based Functionality By Job Role

Unlike many of our competitors, we actually started as a totally different business, a small software development agency that as it grew hit the exact same issues and problems that any growing business does. In our case, we built Teamwork Projects to help ourselves and it turned out (much to our surprise at the time) that our clients and other companies found it useful.

 

At Teamwork.com, we’d like to think that we have a lot of expertise in this area and, so far, we’ve been successful in helping hundreds of thousands of businesses around the world find their organizational zen. Our flagship product Teamwork Projects can cater for businesses at every stage of project planning right through to completion. We have more than 80% of the requested features shown in the second graphic and we do those exceptionally well. The Task Management feature as well as Time Tracking (done in the online app or on our awesome separate desktop timer!) are probably two of most important features for us to use as a company, so we can fully appreciate the need for these and we’re constantly working to improve them.

 

We know how important is it to support companies during the transition from offline to online – getting started can be scary so our in-house support team is on hand 24/7 if you need help or advice along the way. Weekly webinars and help docs are also available and we’ve got hundreds of videos you can check out on our YouTube channel . That being said, our software is one of the easier to use platforms on the market today, but we know it’s always nice to have these options just in case. There is a host of third party integrations too, so you can use your favorite apps alongside us.

Reasons For Using a Project Management Tool

As a parting word, I think the future of Project Management Software is very promising. Educating potential users on benefits of using tools like these is key. At Teamwork.com, we are fortunate to have such great interactions with our users and when we get an out of the blue email telling you how much of a difference Teamwork projects has made to their business, it’s very encouraging. A project manager’s job is to manage the resources on a project and Teamwork Projects is a tried and tested way of doing this.
