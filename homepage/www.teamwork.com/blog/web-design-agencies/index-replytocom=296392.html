Web Design Agencies: Favorite Teamwork Projects Features

Posted July 16, 2015

Welcome to the second post in a three-part series dedicated to the wonderful Web Design Agencies using Teamwork Projects to help run their businesses. In our last issue, we discussed why Web Design Agencies choose Teamwork Projects. (Update: Part 3 is now available) Today, we’re going to concentrate on Web Design Agencies favorite features of Teamwork Projects. All of the companies we spoke to use Teamwork Projects in customized ways that best suit their internal and customer needs. We learned what their favorite things are about Teamwork Projects and works well for them. Let’s read what they had to say.

Q: What are your favorite features or things about Teamwork Projects?

Debbie Levitt, Founder & CEO of Ptype UX Consultancy

A: It has made us so productive! From the administrative side of things especially. We used to rely on e-mails, way back, and things would get lost. I’ve been in this business for 20 years and I’ve tried so many different project management systems. I’ve tried so many ways to organize files, people, communication, projects, and finally I feel like the answer is- Put it on Teamwork Projects. To get opinions on things or to make decisions, the answer is simply: Put it on Teamwork Projects, and that way I don’t have to worry about all of these other systems of communication where conversations are messy and can get lost. The easiest thing for us to do is to keep it all on one great system. It’s great having one medium where if anyone needs to see any information on a project they will find it right away. All files, conversations – everything can be looked at. The amount of organization there is really helpful. A lot of my staff, who are freelancers, have even opened up their own personal accounts to help organize their other stuff.  Our productivity has risen by about 70-90% since we started using Teamwork Projects. The software can’t make my work better, but it can make my collaboration, organization, timing, resourcing, and billing so much better!

To me, the best testimonial of any system is: Can I get my team to use it? Are people adopting it? Are the people in my company using it without me having to beat them over the head? I don’t have to bully anyone into using Teamwork Projects. They just get on with it. I guess because it’s so useful and user-friendly. People just seem to naturally like it. Nobody needs a tutorial because it’s easy to figure it out. I don’t even have to train people on it. They use it. That’s the real testimonial of a good tool: people using it without you telling them to.

One of my personal favorite features is the Gantt Chart. I plot all of my projects against each other. I made an internal project and I inputted all of my tasks and everything in this internal project and that helps me to plot all of my work on one chart. This really helps me to organize and realize what my team and I are doing. Seeing all the projects against each other allows me to see all of projects’ overlay – that really helps me organize my time and resource my people. I love the Gantt Charts. I’m probably using them in a way that wasn’t maybe intended on, but that’s the great thing about Teamwork Projects, there’s no strict way of doing things you can use it whatever way you want.

Another thing I absolutely love is the Tagging. I didn’t even know I wanted Tags until you guys brought it out and now I don’t know what I’d do without them.

The calendar is great for people to mark off when they are on holidays or at business events, and that way I can schedule the workload evenly onto available people.

We don’t share offices or time zones in Ptype, so we can’t just swivel our chairs around to discuss ideas or projects. All of our working communication is on Teamwork Projects. Time zones and things like that can be tricky to work around if you’re just using things like e-mail and phone calls but with Teamwork Projects all the information for a project is in one place. Almost everything we do is done through Teamwork Projects, which also means that there’s a great record of it. Things like paper trails are high on my list because they just help everything. They help you understand how long things take versus how much money they cost etc. Some clients might be like “I never agreed to that”, but with Teamwork Projects everything is written down and documented, so you know exactly where everyone stands.

I’m trying to use as much of Teamwork Projects as I can. I just want to try everything out. Like with tags, I didn’t think I needed them and now I’m like: I need more colors. To be honest, I can’t think of anything that I don’t like about this software. I’m a super happy customer!

Mat Bennett, Managing Director of OKO Digital

A: The Time Logging that Teamwork Projects has is really important to us because we can see which part of the business is really important to us. This tells us which parts are paying for the work we’re putting in and which parts aren’t. Time Tracking was the last straw that made me switch from Basecamp to Teamwork Projects, because it’s so essential when running a successful business. It’s very easy to sit there online and be so busy all day, but when you start tying that back to where the time has gone and where the money has gone- that’s when things become very obvious.

Maybe this is a bit of a dull answer, but my favorite feature in Teamwork Projects is definitely Tasks. Everything stems from it. We do several different types of work, so we can’t just have one rigid structure of how to do things. We’re a Web Design Agency and, therefore, every project we do will be different. Tasks are really flexible, we can have subtasks, dependent tasks, and recurring tasks, so we can spend time doing the work rather than organizing it.

The support that Teamwork.com offers is second to none. It’s one of the main reasons that we love you. You are so open to requests and comments from your users.

We love the product and we have already recommended it to a lot of other companies over the years – and we will continue to do.

Mark Kemp, Managing Director of EQ Design

A: Everything about Time Logging, Tracking and Reporting is really helpful. We love the ease of which you can fill in the Time Sheets, and how there’s so many ways of doing it- It’s so flexible. I like the way I can produce Time Reports in certain ways. Any of the reporting functions, I really like. The ability to work out people’s rates is a very interesting feature. I need to look into that more, but I really like that. Reporting and time sheets, from my point of view, are the main strengths of Teamwork Projects.

Any customer feedback or notes we get from meetings or e-mails, we put into Teamwork Projects. It’s great because nothing gets lost or forgotten and all the important information is in one place. If somebody is on holiday and has an accident, we can cover their work by simply going into their projects and picking up where they left off, because the proposal is up there as well, along with the time logs it’s very easy to raise an invoice for our clients with that information.

For bigger projects, we put an overview message of how long we intend to budget for different things, so really we use it for a transparency reason. That the whole team has access to the timeline of the project makes it so easy to look back and see what’s been done and if things are taking longer than they should it will become clear straight away with the help of Teamwork Projects.

We have and we will continue to recommend Teamwork Projects to other companies. It’s a great product and we find it really helps us to be more productive.

Inge Haverkamp, Optimization & Quality Assurance Manager of MixCom Media Group

A: The templates are the best! I have set up task lists that we can import at the beginning of a project, which is great. This way nobody can forget the little things that always turn out to be important but are only noticed when forgotten. I also love the way Teamwork.com is growing and improving and still the support desk replies to questions quickly and they are always very enthusiastic when we send ideas for new features.

Right now, the new tags feature is probably my favorite feature, and not just because I love the colors. I’m still searching for the best way to use the tags, like certain standard tags for projects and stages. I just saw a video on Teamwork.com’s Facebook page about this, which has given me some new ideas to try.

I would recommend Teamwork Projects to other Web Design Agencies because it provides so many more features than any other project management tool and for such a good price. Lots of tools only have basic features and I really wanted to decrease the number of tools needed on a daily basis. Teamwork Projects gives me that and much more.
