Meet The Interns: Anthony

Posted April 17, 2015

Hey, I’m Anthony. I’m 23 years old and currently in third year of Computer Science in UCC and this is my Meet the Interns post. I am an intern here at Teamwork.com for the next four months. I’ve been in college for the last five years. Before Computer Science in UCC, I was studying Game Design and Development. In that time, I developed a miniature game in Ruby with a friend of mine. You can find the game here.

My love for computers started very late: about 16- when my PS2 broke due to dust issues. I took it apart one day and cleaned it up. When I saw all the pieces to the PS2, my curiosity began. I always like to know how things work and why they do in a particular way. In my free time you can find me playing my PS4, fixing computers and laptops, and supporting Manchester United.

Before I applied for the position in Teamwork.com, I did my research on the company. The story of Peter Coppinger and Daniel Mackey from Digital Crew to Teamwork.com is inspiring. To see a Cork based business do so well in a field of great interest to me is a motivator. When I found out I got the interview, I was excited to see the offices and boy I was not let down! We were greeted by DC who showed us around the offices. They brought in a caterer for our interview. It was unusual, but strangely comforting. I’ve always been nervous at interviews, meetings, and presentations, but somehow I felt right at home here. They brought us into the games room and we played Bomberman while we waited for the interviews. Food, games, and big leather sofas – This was different I thought. One-by-one, we were interviewed. I found out that night that I got the job. I have never been more excited and relieved in my life.

Trying to tell my friends and family about my first week in Teamwork.com was difficult. They told me I must be lying and there was no way I was telling the truth. It’s just been so incredible! The week leading up to the beginning of my work placement, I was quite nervous. Starting a new job can be quite daunting. Walking into the offices for the first time since the interview about six months ago was quite an experience. It was hectic, there was so many new faces. The environment was very welcoming just like the interview. We met the team, a few of whom were over from their respective countries for the week. Colm (another intern from UCC) and I were shown our office. I had no idea I would even be getting an office. Quick specs of the machine I’m using:

  • Intel i7-4770 @ 3.4GHz
  • 16GB of RAM
  • 3 x 24 inch Benq monitors
  • GeForce GTX 760

 

Peter called everyone to the games room for a quick meeting. He welcomed us to Teamwork.com and explained what was happening this week. They were releasing their third product to the market, Teamwork Desk. A product launch in my first week – I knew it was going to be an interesting first week. We met our mentor for the week, MJ, who was going to be guiding us through testing. Testing is a right of passage for all newbies. It helps familiarise yourself with the products. All week we were testing the new website look/design and Teamwork Desk. Thursday arrived and the big launch. Looking at the previous day’s logs, seeing people doing work until the early hours of the next day – I knew from that moment that Teamwork.com is where I want to be.

It was about 2:40pm, the day I had my first company product launch (even though I had little/zero input). It was a day I’ll never forget. Finishing an assignment in college is very satisfactory, but looking around at the team seeing their creation live and how thrilled they looked was much better. The product and website was live. I stayed at the Teamwork.com office that day for the launch party. The expression ‘Work hard, play hard’ comes to mind. An unbelievable party filled with food, alcohol, and impressive dance moves from MJ. Friday morning quickly came, a bit too quickly, but being the Intern on my first week it is important to make a lasting impression. I arrived early, with a bad headache (self-inflicted, of course) to get back down to work. I went in my office and found that someone left me a cocktail on my desk from the night before, as a subtle reminder to never drink again. There was quite a clean up involved, we started to clean but we were way out of our depth. We spent our day helping the support guys on answering queries. We’re using the help and support tools to provide help and support to people who need help and support using the help and support tools – mind blown. It was a hectic, but memorable, first week in Teamwork.com. A remarkable week comes to a close, I’ve never experienced work quite like this. I hope it lasts!
