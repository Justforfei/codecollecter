Make the Switch to Standing Desks

Posted June 3, 2015

Every so often, a new productivity trend or fad comes into fashion. Of late, standing desks seem to be the new ‘it’ thing in the cool and happening offices. Have you made the switch to standing desks in your office?

We’ve heard the reports explaining the revolutionary trend taking businesses by storm. A switch to standing desks promises increased productivity and unwavering health benefits which begs the question, why didn’t we think of standing before? Some research yielded the surprise that standing desks are far from a new fad. Winston Churchill, Ernest Hemingway, and Benjamin Franklin all worked at standing desks and history books can attest to their productivity! Standing and productivity seem to have been linked together for a long time.

Getting started

Standing will take time to get used to and, yes, it will be uncomfortable in the beginning. Your legs will tire and you’ll miss sinking into your comfy black leather chair. But once you get used to being on your feet, you’ll feel like a young Kevin Bacon in Footloose and it’ll be hard to get you to sit down again.

The trick to converting to standing is not to rush it. Start by standing for half-hour intervals. Rest your legs, build your stamina, and incrementally increase the length period of your standing. If you try to stand for the entire day on your first day you will burn out. Your legs will ache and the uncomfort will inhibit productivity. It will take you a while to stand all day, every day. Maybe you’ll stand for periods of the day and sit for tasks that don’t require too much thought like e-mail, etc… Find your own rhythm. Keep a chair or stool close by, use it at intervals, and don’t tire out. The point of standing desks is to make you work better, not to make you uncomfortable or exhausted. Tip: An appropriate standing mat can help prevent fatigue. 

 

Productivity

You release more energy by standing which, in turn, stimulates brain activity. If you think of it simply, your brain and your body are in sync so if the body slows down so does the brain. However, if the body is active, neurons are sent to the brain and the speed of your thought processes increases.

Do you ever need to go for a run when you have a problem that you need to concentrate on? Do you ever have to leave your desk and walk around to try to keep up with your thoughts? Do you tap your foot or fidget when you’re focusing on problem solving? This is because you have built up energy that you have to release. Your brain and your body want to match each other’s activity levels. Standing will regulate and optimise your productivity by allowing configuration between mind and body. You’ll actually be able to concentrate harder and longer if you stand because your energy levels are ripe when you stand.

Health

Is sitting the new smoking?

The average person sleeps for seven hours and sits from nine to 13 hours every day. Twenty out of 24 hours of sedentary activity is not what we are built for.

Studies show that sitting for long periods of the day is hazardous for your health. There are many connections between sedentary behaviour and poor health. Amongst some of the biggest problems associated with sedentary lifestyles are:

  • Heart disease
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity
  • Muscle pain

Sitting isn’t the best position to be in all day. It doesn’t take a genius to understand that running, exercising, and, yes, standing is better for you than sitting down. Take a stand and make a change. (Pun absolutely intended).

Conclusion

The most important thing you do at work is work. Whatever methods help you work to the best of your ability should be available to you. Be it standing, sitting, walking, running, or even a combination of all four. Whatever helps you helps your business too!

The jury is in here at Teamwork.com. Increased productivity and health benefits have us converted! We believe in the standing desk movement. In the wise words of Bob Marley, we implore you to Get Up, Stand Up – and switch to standing desks.
