Time Tracking, How to Not Hate It

Posted June 24, 2015

Guest Post by Nick Johnson

What is the sum of three Mississippi’s, two alligators, and five crocodiles? Ten seconds! Who says time tracking has to be so bad?

What Went Wrong

At SurgeFront, we love time tracking! It increases productivity and efficiency in all types of business. However, it is very difficult to motivate employees to get on board. We’ll catch you up from the beginning of time to explain why many people loathe time tracking.

  • 230 million minutes ago (1577) the minute hand was invented.
  • 67 million minutes ago (1888) the workplace time tracking clock was invented.
  • 66 million minutes ago (1890s) management engineers were invented. They made the corporation (corpulus- body of people) a machine with focus on time efficiency and if-this-then-that reward.
  • The last 66 million minutes (125 years) minimal innovation of management engineering established time tracking.

Basically, people hate to track their time because we stopped innovating.

How We Fixed It

This problem can be solved! We can motivate employees to track time by considering them as valued contributors, and innovating the time tracking process with intrinsic motivators. Thanks to science, consultants understand people better than management engineers did.

Studies show that if a job is thoughtless, it works to automate, or to incentivize the employee with a carrot. However, traditional mechanistic rewards such as bonuses, recognition’s, and gifts can decrease productivity in tasks that require even minimal creativity. Dan Pink, a renowned business author, says, “There is a mismatch between what science knows, and what business does.”

Thankfully, there is a better method to inspire employees to track time! This method will not only inspire your employees to work hard, but enable them to work smart. They will better collaborate as a group and better perform as individuals.

Innovate with intrinsic motivation

The key to time tracking and increasing overall employee effectiveness is to tap into intrinsic motivators. A February Wall Street Journal article reported, “Meaning and purpose is a ‘fallow asset’ that firms can tap to boost staff loyalty and engagement”.

Science shows that intrinsic motivators are the best type of motivators for people performing cognitively challenging tasks, which in today’s world includes most white collar workers. Dan Pink categorizes three intrinsic motivators that can be applied to time tracking:

  1. Autonomy
  2. Mastery
  3. Purpose

Apply intrinsic motivators to time tracking

At SurgeFront, we motivate our employees to track time by involving them in project management. Our meetings, including our prioritization meetings, help all to link what needs to be done to our intrinsic motivations. Time tracking via Teamwork.com is crucial to promoting our culture of self-drive. Here’s how:

Autonomy

Collaborate with employees to help them build with Teamwork Projects:

  • Create milestones, task lists, and tasks.
  • Determine deadlines, priority, and time estimation.
  • Empower employees by allowing them to assign themselves tasks according to interest or expertise.

As project builders on Teamwork.com, our people feel a self-responsibility to effectively execute their plans.

Use Teamwork.com’s three time tracking options according to employee preference:

  • Stopwatch Approach – Start, pause, and stop timer while working on specific task. (Sorry, no reverse, slow-mo, or fast forward option).
  • Task Retrospective Approach – Easily record start and stop time when specific task is completed.
  • Day Retrospective Approach – Easily record various start and stop times for different tasks at the end of day.

Also, our employees can add and track new tasks whenever the need arises.

Mastery

Our people master what they do by tracking time via Teamwork Projects by:

  • Learning principles of project management: assessing project process, task difficulty, and employee ability.
  • Assessing personal ability and improvement by viewing report of past performance and accomplishments.
  • Budgeting time while viewing all tasks and time estimations assigned to them in the “Dashboard” view of Teamwork Projects.
  • Hitting deadlines and milestones.

Purpose

Our people know that every minute of their time matters to them, to us, and to our clients. Each player is important on the team. Instead of perspective-blind employees, Teamwork Projects shows our people how every minute tracked fits into the company’s projects. Furthermore, the software is customized with our brand, colors, and logo in order to promote company relevancy and loyalty.

Conclusion:

Let’s throw out the sticks and carrots. In an ever changing world, we must innovate our time tracking strategy by applying a greater purpose to it. Let us use time tracking to enable our employees to gain autonomy, mastery, and purpose so that we can all work harder and smarter as a team. That is the bottom line.
