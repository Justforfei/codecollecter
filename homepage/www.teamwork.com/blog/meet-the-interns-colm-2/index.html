Meet The Interns: Colm, Take 2

Posted July 17, 2015

Hey there, Colm here again. I am more than halfway through an internship here at Teamwork.com and this Meet the Interns post is insight into how I work and some other stuff about the man behind the devilishly handsome photo you see above.

How I Work

As I already mentioned Teamwork.com is not a 9-to-5 style environment; we all work differently. Personally, I often start at home in the morning. This could mean checking on tasks that may have been assigned to me by earlier birds, or simply getting a sense of what I will work on. Teamwork Chat Beta is a fantastic tool for this,  it’s informal and tasks or assignments can be created directly from the interface. It also lets me gauge the lay of the land and if there had been any important activity overnight.

Once I’m at my desk, I will usually check Teamwork.com. I look for little changes or announcements that may have been posted, as this gives me a starting point at the office. If there is milk, I have coffee. If there is cinnamon pastries, then I have them too!

I work with music mostly, but only instrumental, as it gives me a focus and helps to lock me in to the tasks at hand. The software that we develop here is the tool that we use to get stuff done. It’s simple, keeps me on track, and is a great way to prioritize what you have to get done.

We work together and for each other here, so my day will also have countless little interactions, either seeking, or offering help. I try to end each day with an accomplishment, such as something that I had planned to do and have ticked off a task list. This is how I build towards goals, with tiny bites.

That’s how I work and below is a little more about who I am, in question form!

1. Tell me three facts about yourself.

  • I’m a Kerryman.
  • I tell people that I’m a Kerryman too much.
  • I was actually born in Cork.

2. What are your greatest strengths and weaknesses?

Strengths
  • I have a strong sense that I could be good at most things I turn my hand to, a perceived confidence in myself.
  • I am almost compelled to take risks and question everything with varying degrees of success and injury.
Weakness
  • I am my own worst critic at times.
  • I find it difficult to say no, often agreeing to do things for others that puts pressure on my own endeavours.

3. What have you learned from Teamwork.com?

I have learned above all that the environment here drives creation. The more natural I feel at ‘work’ the more natural it feels to ‘work’. It erodes the feeling of wanting to get the school uniform off and instead serves as a reminder that we are all here doing what we actually want to do. Hawaiian shirt or suit and tie!

4. What do you like about your role?

I love that we are encouraged to learn here. That might mean taking a few minutes to watch a youtube video to solve a problem or learn a skill. I also love the discussion that we have regarding topics that are related to Teamwork.com. There is a wider world at large and I love how I feel a part of that here.

5. What motivates you?

I am almost solely motivated by ambition, not for money or fame but by standing behind something that I am proud of. Everyday we help create products here at Teamwork.com that are in peoples hands from Blackpool to Taiwan. This is a huge motivation and one that Teamwork.com itself shares. We help people, essentially.

6. What are your goals and aspirations for the future?

My goals are to gain as much experience and practice so that I can feel I have contributed to my own life. My aspiration is simply to be content. Heavy :P.

7. What are your hobbies outside of work?

Outside of work I love to travel and to scare the living daylights out of myself. I push the Do Not Push Button, habitually. I love to watch live music and to dance although the dancing may result in injury to myself or others if approached. I like to take photographs, program, and to play with Adobe products.

8. If you could high-five one person, living or not, who would it be?

I would choose the American astronomer, cosmologist, astrophysicist and science popularizer, Carl Sagan. For small creatures such as we, the vastness is bearable only through love.

9. What would you do if you were the one survivor in a plane crash?

I imagine there would be weeping followed by wallowing. With that out of the way, I would pretend that I was in the TV series Lost. Then choose a particularly juicy passenger for supper :).

10. Who would win in a fight between Superman and Batman?

Batman, naturally.

11. Describe the color yellow to somebody who’s blind.

Its the soft smooth Am7 of a jazz saxophone lazily drifting to your ears with the summer sun warm on your eyelids.

12. What actor would star in the movie of your life?

Jake Gyllenhaal, I have loved him since Donnie Darko and he has rarely put a foot wrong in his career, plus he is devilishly handsome.

With that said, you should have a little more of an understanding of who I am, what I do and what makes me tick and indeed, tock.

Until the next time, adios.

Colm.
