How Web Design Agencies Use Teamwork Projects

Posted July 30, 2015

This is the final post in a three-part series (Check out Part 1 & Part 2) dedicated to the wonderful Web Design Agencies who use Teamwork Projects. Web Design Agencies, like any other industry, face many challenges in their work. In this post, we delve into some of those obstacles and talked to our users about how they use Teamwork Projects to overcome them. Here’s what our customers had to say.

What are the Main Challenges in Your Industry?

Debbie Levitt, Founder & CEO of Ptype UX Consultancy

I think the biggest ones, especially the ones which Teamwork.com helps us solve (because you can’t make my actual work better) are organisation and communication. Organisation is both internal and external to our company– in terms of dealing with the client and expectations and making sure that they understand what they’re getting and when. In many cases as well, clearly defining the boundaries of a project because that’s certainly when things can go wrong for web designers. Having everything in Teamwork Projects allows you to have a paper trail reference as to what the boundaries are. People lose e-mails, so we just love having Teamwork Projects as our internal and external paper trail of anything anyone has ever thought, said, decided on another things like that.

Mark Kemp, Managing Director of EQ Design

Ensuring companies value the skills and experience of good web designers and developers. As this is the final post in the series, it concentrates on how Web Design Agencies use Teamwork Projects. So, let’s hear from some of our customers.

Mat Bennett, Managing Director of OKO Digital

The level of competition, how much commoditization has happened and the level of work.

Inge Haverkamp, Optimization & Quality Assurance Manager of MixCom Media Group

Within a big company that has departments in design, development, marketing, and publishing, it can be a challenge to stay involved in projects at the right times. We work in teams with team managers, but some projects span across teams. When working in Teamwork Projects, it is much easier to stay involved in every project, receive tasks with all the information you need at the right time, and send a message or just a ‘like’ instead of an e-mail.

Teamwork Projects is all about streamlining your business so that you can concentrate on your work and not the administration side of it! Managing projects and tasks shouldn’t be a nightmare. It should be simple and efficient. Use Teamwork Projects to get the most out of your business. See how some of our clients are using it.

How do you use Teamwork Projects?

Inge Haverkamp, Optimization & Quality Assurance Manager of MixCom Media Group

Right now, we’ve got about 19 projects running in Teamwork Projects. It became clear that our old way of project management wasn’t working, and Teamwork Projects made that even clearer, which is good, because this way we can actually change our workflow and make projects run smoother. I started with making task lists in templates, which means that basically every standard todo is in a template so it won’t be overlooked. We also have a template project in which I place a few ‘template’ notebooks that I want in every project. This way, every project has the same structure and particular information is always at the same place, which is really helpful if someone gets involved with a project later on or needs to know something quickly. Finally, with milestones, we decide which task lists are up next. We’re also looking at Teamwork Desk and Chat, which are both looking interesting for the future.

Mark Kemp, Managing Director of EQ Design

So, when we get a new project from a client, we create a new unique number for each project. I think we’re up to something like 11,000 right now. We put up the brief, and then people are invited to get a hold of that brief. Then the Project Manager of that job sets up some initial tasks, people do the work, they respond to the message they need to. We are quite strict in what files get uploaded to Teamwork Projects. Only the agreed upon signed proposal goes up, none of the negotiating proposals, just so it’s easy for someone who wasn’t involved in negotiations to know exactly what files to work with and there’s no confusion as to which files or versions of files need to be used.

We invite our clients to use Teamwork Projects, some of them use it, some of them don’t. It’s handy for all of us because all information is kept in one place. We use restricted privacy when sharing things with our clients since our clients don’t need access to things like messages or time logs so it’s nice that Teamwork Projects has the privacy features. It’s great when they have loads of files to send us and they don’t have dropbox they can just upload them on Teamwork Projects.

Debbie Levitt, Founder & CEO of Ptype UX Consultancy

We put all of our projects as Task lists into one Internal Project. That way our clients won’t see anything they’re not supposed to and we’re able to make up a Gantt Chart on our progress too. Everything everyone needs to do on a project will be in one place. I’ve got everything tagged, everyone puts in time. I’ll upload a brief in messages, and then I’ll upload files like the style guide from the clients too.

A lot of our clients have their own Teamwork Project accounts, so I can hook up to their accounts and bring back some information back to our own projects.

We use notebooks for things like information we find on how to better on time management, what projects are potentially coming down the road for us.

Gantt Charts really help me to organise my time. I plot all of my projects against each other. I made an internal project and I inputted all of my tasks and everything in this internal project and that helps me to plot all of my work on one chart. This really helps me to organize and realize what I’m doing and my team of course. Seeing all the projects against each other allows me to see all of projects overlay that really helps me organise my time and resource my people. I love the Gantt Charts. I’m probably using them in a way that wasn’t maybe intended on, but that’s the great thing about Teamwork Projects, there’s no strict way of doing things you can use it however way you want.

Mat Bennett, Managing Director of OKO Digital

We’re not the type of company that sits on a big pile of money. So, if we want to change things, that sadly means working really hard, so every bit of efficiency helps. We tend to either work on quite sizeable projects or lots of small ones – and I think those are the two extreme examples where productivity tools really come into their own. With big projects, Teamwork Projects helps to keep sight over all of the details, and smaller projects are more about organizing and making sure everything gets enough time and gets done.

Manage the work process. We can work in cycles and track notes. We can also work with our clients and publishers. Ticking jobs off and getting things done. Manage time, efficiency, and effectiveness.
